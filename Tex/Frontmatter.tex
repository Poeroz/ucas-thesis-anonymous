语音到语音生成旨在实现从源语音到目标语音的直接转换，涵盖语音翻译和语音对话等典型任务，是构建自然人机交互系统的核心技术。该领域的技术路线经历了从三阶段级联到两阶段级联再到端到端模型的持续演进。端到端方法最直接的思路是从输入语音表示直接映射到输出语音表示，但语音信号的连续性和复杂性使得这一映射关系难以学习，且语音模态的平行数据远比文本稀缺。因此，本文的核心策略是借助文本生成来引导语音生成——通过先生成目标文本再基于文本表示生成语音的方式进行任务解耦，在降低建模难度的同时追求高质量、低延迟的实时语音交互。本文围绕四个递进的科学问题展开系统性研究，共完成五项具体工作。

端到端语音到语音翻译模型通常需要稀缺的平行语音翻译数据从头训练，而语音到文本翻译和语音合成领域各自已积累了大量高质量的预训练模型。然而，不同模型使用不同的词表和表示空间，S2TT模型的输出无法直接作为TTS模型的输入。针对这一预训练模型的组合与利用问题，本文提出基于词表转换的组合式语音到语音翻译模型（Composable Speech-to-Speech Translation, ComSpeech）。ComSpeech设计了基于连接时序分类的词表转换器，打通了语音到文本翻译模型与语音合成模型之间的接口壁垒，使任意预训练模型可自由组合为完整的语音到语音翻译系统，并支持零样本模式，无需平行语音翻译数据即可构建端到端系统。

在端到端可行的基础上，自回归解码面临语音序列过长导致的高延迟问题，而已有非自回归方法虽然解码速度快，但翻译质量与自回归模型存在较大差距。针对这一建模能力与解码效率的矛盾，本文提出基于有向无环图的语音到语音翻译模型（Directed Acyclic Transformer for Speech-to-Speech Translation, DASpeech）。DASpeech采用两阶段非自回归解码框架，以有向无环图结构建模目标序列的多种可能路径，有效缓解了非自回归翻译的多峰性问题，在翻译质量上达到甚至超过自回归模型的水平，同时实现了高达18.53倍的解码加速。

进入以大语言模型为核心的仅解码器架构后，研究范围从翻译拓展到通用语音对话，串行的文本-语音生成方式导致响应延迟过高。针对多模态序列的同步流式生成问题，本文首先提出LLaMA-Omni，设计基于连接时序分类的非自回归流式语音解码器，在大语言模型生成文本的同时并行预测离散语音单元，实现低延迟的实时语音交互，响应延迟低至226毫秒。在此基础上，提出LLaMA-Omni 2，引入基于读写交替策略的自回归流式语音解码器，通过门控融合机制自适应地利用大语言模型的隐状态和文本词元嵌入，在保持实时交互的同时大幅提升语音的内容准确性和自然度。进一步地，上述模型均面向基于轮次的对话模式，无法应对持续输入的语音流。针对双向语音流的统一序列建模问题，本文提出LLaMA-Omni-Duplex，将用户语音、模型回复文本和模型回复语音三个信道按固定比例组织成交织序列，统一由大语言模型进行自回归建模，通过文本信道中的特殊标记将全双工场景下的时机决策转化为下一词元预测问题，无需额外的分类模块，在轮替和打断场景上均取得了较高的成功率和回复质量。

\intobmk\chapter*{Abstract}% 显示在书签但不显示在目录

Speech-to-speech generation aims to directly convert source speech into target speech, encompassing tasks such as speech translation and spoken dialogue, and serves as a core technology for building natural human-computer interaction systems. The technical approaches in this field have evolved from three-stage cascaded systems to two-stage cascaded systems and further to end-to-end models. The most straightforward end-to-end approach is to directly map input speech representations to output speech representations, but the continuous and complex nature of speech signals makes this mapping extremely difficult to learn, and parallel data in the speech modality is far scarcer than in text. Therefore, the core strategy of this thesis is to leverage text generation to guide speech generation --- decomposing the task by first generating target text and then generating speech based on the text representation, reducing modeling difficulty while pursuing high-quality, low-latency real-time speech interaction. This thesis conducts systematic research on four progressive scientific problems, resulting in five concrete works.

End-to-end speech-to-speech translation models typically require scarce parallel speech translation data for training from scratch, while the speech-to-text translation and text-to-speech synthesis fields have each accumulated abundant high-quality pretrained models. However, different models use different vocabularies and representation spaces, making S2TT model outputs incompatible with TTS model inputs. To address this problem of pretrained model composition and utilization, we propose ComSpeech (Composable Speech-to-Speech Translation), which designs a CTC-based vocabulary adaptor that bridges the interface gap between S2TT and TTS models, enabling flexible composition of arbitrary pretrained models into a complete speech-to-speech translation system with support for zero-shot settings.

Building upon the feasibility of end-to-end translation, autoregressive decoding faces high latency due to the excessive length of speech sequences, while existing non-autoregressive methods achieve fast decoding but with significant quality degradation. To address this contradiction between modeling capacity and decoding efficiency, we propose DASpeech (Directed Acyclic Transformer for Speech-to-Speech Translation), which adopts a two-stage non-autoregressive decoding framework with directed acyclic graph structure to model multiple possible paths of the target sequence, effectively alleviating the multi-modality problem in non-autoregressive translation, achieving translation quality comparable to or better than autoregressive models with up to 18.53x decoding speedup.

Moving to decoder-only architectures centered on large language models, the research scope expands from translation to general spoken dialogue, where sequential text-then-speech generation leads to unacceptable response latency. To address synchronous streaming generation of multimodal sequences, we first propose LLaMA-Omni, which designs a CTC-based non-autoregressive streaming speech decoder that predicts discrete speech units in parallel while the large language model generates text, achieving real-time speech interaction with response latency as low as 226 milliseconds. Building upon this, we propose LLaMA-Omni 2, which introduces an autoregressive streaming speech decoder based on a read-write interleaving strategy, adaptively fusing the hidden states and text token embeddings of the large language model through a gating mechanism, significantly improving speech accuracy and naturalness while maintaining real-time interaction. Furthermore, the above models all operate in turn-based dialogue mode and cannot handle continuous speech streams. To address unified sequence modeling of bidirectional speech streams, we propose LLaMA-Omni-Duplex, which organizes user speech, model response text, and model response speech into an interleaved sequence at a fixed ratio, unified under autoregressive modeling by the large language model. Special tokens in the text channel convert timing decisions in full-duplex scenarios into next-token prediction without additional classification modules, achieving high success rates in both turn-taking and interruption scenarios along with strong response quality.
