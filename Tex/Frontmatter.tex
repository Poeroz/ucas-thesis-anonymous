语音到语音生成旨在实现从源语音到目标语音的直接转换，涵盖语音翻译和语音交互等典型任务，是构建自然人机交互系统的核心技术。该领域的模型结构经历了从级联架构到编码器-解码器架构，再到以大语言模型为核心的仅解码器架构的持续演进。本文沿这一结构演进主线，针对四个关键科学问题展开系统性研究，共完成五项具体工作。

在编码器-解码器架构下，本文围绕高效高质量的语音到语音翻译展开研究。针对建模能力与解码效率的矛盾，提出DASpeech，采用有向无环图Transformer建模目标序列的多种可能路径，在非自回归框架下达到与自回归模型相当甚至更优的翻译质量，同时实现大幅度的解码加速。针对预训练模型的组合与利用，提出ComSpeech，通过基于连接时序分类的词表转换器打通语音到文本翻译模型与语音合成模型之间的接口壁垒，使任意预训练模型可自由组合为完整的语音到语音翻译系统，并支持零样本模式。

在仅解码器架构下，本文围绕低延迟高质量的实时语音交互和全双工语音交互展开研究。针对多模态序列的同步流式生成，首先提出LLaMA-Omni，设计基于连接时序分类的非自回归流式语音解码器，在大语言模型生成文本的同时并行预测离散语音单元，实现低延迟的实时语音交互。在此基础上，提出LLaMA-Omni 2，引入基于读写交替策略的自回归流式语音解码器，通过门控融合机制自适应地利用大语言模型的隐状态和文本词元嵌入，在保持实时交互的同时大幅提升语音的准确性和自然度。针对双向语音流的统一序列建模，提出LLaMA-Omni-Duplex，将用户语音、模型回复文本和模型回复语音三个信道按固定比例组织成交织序列，统一由大语言模型进行自回归建模，通过文本信道中的特殊标记将全双工场景下的时机决策转化为下一词元预测问题，在轮替和打断场景上均取得了较高的成功率和回复质量。

\intobmk\chapter*{Abstract}% 显示在书签但不显示在目录

Speech-to-speech generation aims to directly convert source speech into target speech, encompassing tasks such as speech translation and spoken dialogue, and serves as a core technology for building natural human-computer interaction systems. The model architectures in this field have evolved from cascaded systems to encoder-decoder architectures, and further to decoder-only architectures centered on large language models. This thesis follows this architectural evolution and conducts systematic research on four key scientific problems, resulting in five concrete works.

Under the encoder-decoder architecture, this thesis investigates efficient and high-quality speech-to-speech translation. To address the contradiction between modeling capacity and decoding efficiency, we propose DASpeech, which employs directed acyclic graph Transformer to model multiple possible paths of the target sequence, achieving translation quality comparable to or better than autoregressive models under a non-autoregressive framework, with significant decoding speedup. To address the composition and utilization of pretrained models, we propose ComSpeech, which bridges the vocabulary gap between speech-to-text translation and text-to-speech models through a CTC-based vocabulary adaptor, enabling flexible composition of arbitrary pretrained models into a complete speech-to-speech translation system, with support for zero-shot settings.

Under the decoder-only architecture, this thesis investigates low-latency high-quality real-time speech interaction and full-duplex speech interaction. To address the synchronous streaming generation of multimodal sequences, we first propose LLaMA-Omni, which designs a CTC-based non-autoregressive streaming speech decoder that predicts discrete speech units in parallel while the large language model generates text, enabling low-latency real-time speech interaction. Building upon this, we propose LLaMA-Omni 2, which introduces an autoregressive streaming speech decoder based on a read-write interleaving strategy, adaptively fusing the hidden states and text token embeddings of the large language model through a gating mechanism, significantly improving speech accuracy and naturalness while maintaining real-time interaction. To address the unified sequence modeling of bidirectional speech streams, we propose LLaMA-Omni-Duplex, which organizes user speech, model response text, and model response speech into an interleaved sequence at a fixed ratio, unified under autoregressive modeling by the large language model. Special tokens in the text channel convert timing decisions in full-duplex scenarios into next-token prediction, achieving high success rates in both turn-taking and interruption scenarios along with strong response quality.
